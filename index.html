The Babylon Philosophy

"A part of all you earn is yours to keep. It should be not less than a tenth no matter how little you earn."

— The Richest Man in Babylon · George S. Clason

✦

Written in 1926, The Richest Man in Babylon by George S. Clason contains the same financial wisdom that built fortunes then — and still does today. The Babylon Method brings those principles to life as interactive tools.

I

Start thy purse to fattening

Save at least one-tenth of all you earn. Always pay yourself first.

II

Control thy expenditures

Budget what remains. Distinguish needs from desires ruthlessly.

III

Make thy gold multiply

Put savings to work. Every coin saved should earn more coins.

IV

Guard thy gold from loss

Protect your principal. Never risk what you cannot afford to lose.

V

Own thy dwelling

The man who owns the roof over his head has security few others know.
